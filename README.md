An Insidious Takeover by Microplastics

Interactive Data Visualization Project

Overview

This project presents an interactive, data-driven exploration of microplastic pollution and its impact on ecosystems and human health. It integrates environmental datasets, geospatial analysis, and narrative visualization techniques to trace the lifecycle of microplastics — from production and environmental distribution to biological accumulation.

The goal is to transform complex, multi-source environmental data into an engaging and insight-driven digital experience.

Problem Statement

Microplastics are increasingly detected in oceans, rivers, food systems, and even human organs. Despite growing scientific evidence, public awareness remains limited.

This project explores:
How do microplastics travel from everyday products into the human body, and what measurable impact do they have?

Through interactive storytelling and layered visualizations, the project reveals global pathways, concentration patterns, and biological implications of microplastic exposure.

Key Features

Streamgraph visualizing microplastic source flows into water systems

Interactive global map showing concentration clusters in oceans

Heatmap representing dietary microplastic intake by country

Treemap and donut charts illustrating microplastic composition breakdown

Human-body visualization mapping organ-level accumulation

Connected dot plot comparing concentration trends (2016 vs 2024)

Radar chart comparing ingestion patterns across species

Experimental vibrating line chart linking corporate production to environmental impact

Data Sources

The project integrates curated and self-derived datasets, including:

Global plastic export data

Country-wise plastic pollution statistics

Dietary microplastic intake research (2018)

Marine animal ingestion analysis

Human organ microplastic concentration data

Plastic production data from major global companies

Data cleaning and harmonization were performed to ensure consistency across heterogeneous sources.

Design Approach

This project emphasizes narrative-driven visualization, progressive scrollytelling, and interactive exploration rather than static charts. Visual metaphors (such as vibration intensity representing production impact) were used to connect quantitative data with intuitive understanding.

The experience is structured to guide users logically — from pollution sources to ecosystem impact to human health implications.

Results & Insights

Fibers and fragments dominate microplastic composition.

Coastal and densely populated regions show higher concentration clusters.

Fish consumption represents a significant dietary exposure channel.

Microplastic presence in human organs has increased over time.

Corporate plastic production volume strongly correlates with pollution patterns.

The interactive interface allows users to explore findings dynamically instead of consuming static summaries.
